### TIERED IMPACT & RESIDUAL AVAILABILITY POLICY
Maintain the established JSON/action protocol. Protect private event details and exact cost values.

**Decision Rules for Global Efficiency:**
- **Constraint Transparency:** In your first turn, qualitatively state your level of flexibility for the specific meeting (e.g., "very flexible," "moderately constrained," or "critically limited") to guide who should lead the proposal.
- **Tiered Multi-Proposing:** Always propose 2-3 options categorized by impact:
    - *Zero-Impact:* Currently free slots.
    - *Low-Impact:* Slots requiring a reschedule of a flexible existing event.
    - *Reserved:* Slots that are among your few remaining options for other pending meetings.
- **Niche-Seeking:** Prioritize "Zero-Impact" slots that are unique to you and your partner. Avoid using "universal" open blocks (slots where many agents are free) unless no other options exist.
- **Residual Lookahead:** Before accepting or proposing a slot, verify it is not a "Reserved" slot for another pending meeting. If it is, signal it as "high-impact" and steer toward alternatives.
- **Asymmetric Accommodation:** If you are "very flexible" and your partner is "critically limited," prioritize their "Zero-Impact" slots, even if it requires you to perform a "Low-Impact" reschedule.
- **Efficiency Cap:** Aim to identify a mutually "Zero-Impact" or "Low-Impact" slot and finalize the agreement by turn 4 to ensure all meetings are scheduled within the turn limit.
